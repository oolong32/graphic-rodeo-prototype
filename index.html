Idea

A visual excursus on a current discourse.

A Workshop format, intermediating between Art Schools and Practices Partners to find visual answers to relevant questions.

Framework

  • An Art School as Host
  • Students as impulse creators
  • A Practice Partner as topic guide
  • ‹graphic.rodeo› as project enabler

goals

The workshop picks up explosive questions out of the practice and develops innovative visual answers to it.

Goals Students

  • Working along a current topic
  • Gaining practical experience
  • Experimental working
  • Collaborative experience
  • Visibility of one’s own work

Goals Practice Partner

  • Opportunity for topic leadership
  • Benefiting from innovative perspectives
  • Receiving contemporary results
  • Possibility to contribute to a debate
    • gaining public attention

Goals School

  • Playing role as a social initiator
  • Benefiting from practical relevance
  • Engaging in a current discourse
  • Possibility to contribute to a debate
    • gaining public attention

benefits

By making the work visible through publication a shared benefit arises.

Method

The Students get in touch with different, experimental design methods. The Workshop is divided into three differnt working steps:

  • Impression: elementary introduction
  • Expression: experimental phase
  • Modulation:
    • form and content melt to the result

The working steps interlock with each other. At the end of each step, a critique of the results takes place in the plenary. The complexity of the content increases along the working steps.

Procedure

‹graphic.rodeo› as project enabler is takeing up a current topic. It addresses this topic to both; The Partner as well as the School. The topic invites all participants to make their own contribution.

I. Preparation

  • The project will be dimensioned in consultation with the Partner and the School.
  • The Partner procures all necessary information.
  • ‹graphic.rodeo› prepares the essential contents for the lessons.
  • For teaching purposes; ‹graphic.rodeo› defines the media into the results are flowing to.
  • Before the workshop starts, ‹graphic.rodeo› organises an info event. This pre-briefing introduces the subject and the project facility. It's the first get together between the Students and the Partner.

II. Realisation

  • The workshop lasts one week.
  • It takes place in groups/classes.
  • The Workshop will be led by ‹graphic.rodeo› as lecturers and accompanied by the Partner.
  • The last day of the Workshop is dedicated to production and presentation.
  • At the presentation, the work will get reflected and discussed by all participants.

III. Post-production

After the workshop, ‹graphic.rodeo› consolidates all created materials. It processes the results and puts them into a predefined format / medium, e.g. brochure, zine, newspaper, catalouge, poster, animation, etc… This publication will be made available to all participants.

happening

Finally a happening will take place: a vernissage, an exhibition, an installation, a demonstration, a campaign Kick-Off, etc…

Requirements

  • The School is the host of the Workshop.
  • The School provides the infrastructure such as rooms, workshops, working materials.
  • The School compensates ‹graphic.rodeo› as lecturers.
  • The Partner provides the necessary information
  • The Partner pays a contribution to the school for providing the teaching facilities.
  • The Partner compensates ‹graphic.rodeo› for the extraordinary preparatory and post production work.
  • As Co-publishers, the Partner and the School share the costs of the publication.

About us

The Workshop format ‹graphic.rodeo› is an initiative by Rafael Koch from Zurich based design Studio Noir Associates. The Studio intermediates through this project between Art Schools and Practice Partners. For specific tasks, Noir Associates collaborates with fellow Designers. As Co-lecturers, they bring their specific knowledge into the classroom.

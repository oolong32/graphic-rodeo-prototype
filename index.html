de|en

Idea

a visual excursus on a current discourse. A Workshop format, intermediating between Art Schools and Practices Partners, to find visual answers to relevant questions.

Framework

  • An Art School as Host
  • Students as impulse creators
  • A Practice Partner as topic guide
  • ‹ graphic.rodeo › as project enabler

Goals

The workshop picks up explosive questions out of the practice and develops innovative visual answers to it.

Goals for the Students

  • Working along a current topic
  • Gaining practical experience
  • Experimental Working
  • Collaborative Experience
  • Visibility of one’s own work

Goals for the Practice Partner

  • Opportunity for topic leadership
  • Benefiting from innovative perspectives
  • Receiving contemporary results
  • Possibility to contribute to the debate

Goals for the School

  • Playing role as a social initiator
  • Benefiting from practical relevance
  • Engaging in a current discourse
  • Possibility to contribute to the debate

By making the work visible through publication a shared benefit arises.

Method

The Students come into contact with different, experimental Design methods.

The Workshop is divided into working steps:

  • Impression: Informative and elementary introduction
  • Expression: Experimental phase
  • Modulation

The working steps interlock wit each other. At the end of each step, a critique of the results takes place in the plenary. The complexity of the content increases along the working steps.

Procedure

‹g.r› as project enabler is takeing usually up a current topic. It addresses this topic to both; The Partner as well as the School. The topic invites all participants to make their own contribution.

I. Preparation

  • The project will be dimensioned in consultation with the Partner and the School.
  • procures the necessary information.
  • it also prepares the essential contents for the lessons.
  • For teaching purposes; ‹graphic.rodeo› defines the media into the results are flowing to. (The Partner’s and School’s wishes can be still considered at this point.)
  • Before the workshop starts, ‹graphic.rodeo› usually organises an info event for the Students. This pre-briefing introduces the subject, the Partner and the project facility.
  • At the end, the Students receive the briefing.

II. Realisation

  • The Workshop duration is one up to two weeks.
  • It can take place in classes or groups.
  • The Workshop will be led by ‹graphic.rodeo› as lecturers and accompanied by the Partner.
  • The last day of the Workshop is dedicated to production and presentation.
  • At the presentation, the work will get reflected and discussed by all participants.

III. Post-production

After the workshop, ‹graphic.rodeo› consolidates all created materials. It processes the results and puts them into a predefined format/medium, e.g. brochure, zine, newspaper, catalouge, poster, animation, etc.

The publication will be made available to all participants.

Finally a happening will take place: A vernissage, an exhibition, An installation, a demonstration, A campaign kick-off, etc…

Requirements

  • The School is the host of the Workshop.
  • The School provides the infrastructure such as rooms, workshops, working materials.
  • The School compensates ‹graphic.rodeo› as lecturers.
  • The Partner provides the necessary information
  • The Partner pays a contribution to the school for providing the teaching facilities.
  • The Partner compensates ‹g.r› for the extraordinary preparatory and post production.
  • As co-publishers, the Partner and the School share the costs of the publication.
